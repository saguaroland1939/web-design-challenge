Introduction

Phoenix weather made the news this year following a record number of nights that did not fall below 90 degrees. This extreme weather has the community worried about a possible threat to our saguaro plantings around Phoenix and our iconic saguaro forests in surrounding desert areas.

  • Phoenix breaks record for its hottest month, with 16 nights that stayed at or above 90 degrees
  • Will the iconic saguaro cactus start to disappear from parts of the Southwest?

Desert Botanical Garden has conducted an annual Saguaro Inventory since 2012. Every year 20+ volunteers and staff visit each saguaro on the property to assess its condition. Height measurements are taken every 5 years. We obtained the saguaro inventory dataset from DBG and performed basic statistical analyses find out if weather trends over the past several years have had a significant impact on the health of the saguaro population at DBG.

Analyses

We focused on growth rate and mortality rate as two indicators of saguaro health. The health of the population as a whole is related to the age of the saguaros so the first analysis displays the distribution of heights.

  1. How old are the saguaros at the Garden?
  2. Do hotter night-time temperatures correlate with higher saguaro mortality rates in subsequent years?
  3. Do higher precipitation rates correlate with higher saguaro growth rates?

Data

Historical Saguaro Inventory records were provided by Desert Botanical Garden from A Living Collections Management System Weather data was purchased from Visual CrossingSaguaro and weather datasets were cleaned up and reformated using pandas.
