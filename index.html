How Saguaros Respond to Extreme Weather in Phoenix


An Analysis of Eight Years of Saguaro Health Data

Collected at Desert Botanical Garden

Introduction

Phoenix weather made the news a lot this summer and record temperatures have the community worried about a possible threat to our saguaro plantings around Phoenix and our iconic saguaro forests in surrounding desert areas.

  • Phoenix breaks record for its hottest month, with 16 nights that stayed at or above 90 degrees
  • Will the iconic saguaro cactus start to disappear from parts of the Southwest?

Desert Botanical Garden has conducted an annual Saguaro Inventory since 2012. Every year 20+ volunteers and staff visit each saguaro on the property to assess its condition. Height measurements are taken every 5 years. We obtained the full historic dataset from DBG and performed basic statistical analyses find out if weather trends over the past several years have had a significant impact on the health of the saguaro population at DBG.

Research questions

  1. Is there a relationship between saguaro mortality and the number of nights above 90 degrees in a year? Cacti cannot "breathe" when the night-time temperature stays above 90, so we would expect to see higher mortality in years with more nights above 90.
  2. Is there a relationship between saguaro growth rate and precipitation? We would expect to see higher growth rates in the year(s) following a high precipitation year.

Data

  • Historical Saguaro Inventory records were provided by Desert Botanical Garden from A Living Collections Management System
  • Weather data was purchased from Visual Crossing
  • Saguaro and weather datasets were cleaned up and reformated using pandas. The Jupyter Notebooks used to carry out this analysis can be accessed on GitHub
